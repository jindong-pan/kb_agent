## Literature: "How to Take Smart Notes" — Sönke Ahrens (2017)

The book is the definitive introduction to Luhmann's Zettelkasten
method for a modern audience. Ahrens argues that most students and
academics fail not because of lack of ideas, but because of poor
note-taking systems that don't support thinking.

Key argument: writing is not the output of thinking, it is the
medium of thinking. You do not write up ideas after you have them;
you develop ideas by writing them down and connecting them.

The book distinguishes between storage (saving information for later)
and elaboration (connecting information to what you already know).
Most note-taking systems optimize for storage. Zettelkasten optimizes
for elaboration.

Practical takeaway: the bottleneck in knowledge work is not reading
more, it is processing what you read into linked permanent notes.

Source: Ahrens, S. (2017). How to Take Smart Notes. CreateSpace.
