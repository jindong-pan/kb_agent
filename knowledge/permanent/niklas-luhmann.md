## Niklas Luhmann and his Zettelkasten

Niklas Luhmann (1927–1998) was a German sociologist who became one
of the most prolific academics of the 20th century. He attributed
his extraordinary output directly to his Zettelkasten system.

His physical slip-box contained over 90,000 index cards at the time
of his death. Each card had a unique identifier and referenced other
cards, forming a dense network of linked ideas across decades of work.

Luhmann described the Zettelkasten not as a storage system but as
a communication partner. He would write a note, file it, and then
"ask" the system questions by following chains of linked cards —
often discovering unexpected connections between ideas he had written
years apart.
