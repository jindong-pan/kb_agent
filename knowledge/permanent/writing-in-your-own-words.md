## Writing in your own words forces understanding

The rule in Zettelkasten is to never copy-paste from a source.
Every permanent note must be written entirely in your own words,
as if explaining to someone who has never read the source.

This constraint is the system's most powerful feature. The act
of rewriting forces you to actually understand the idea rather
than just recognize it. If you cannot explain it in your own
words, you do not yet understand it.

A direct quote tells you nothing about your comprehension.
A paraphrase proves it.
