## Zettelkasten vs folder hierarchies

Traditional note-taking organizes ideas into folders and subfolders —
a tree structure where each note belongs to exactly one category.
This forces you to decide where an idea "lives" at the moment of
capture, before you know how you will use it.

Zettelkasten uses links instead of folders. A note about "attention
mechanisms" can link to machine learning notes, neuroscience notes,
and philosophy-of-mind notes simultaneously. It belongs to all of
them because ideas do not fit into single categories.

The practical difference: in a folder system, you retrieve notes
by remembering where you filed them. In a Zettelkasten, you retrieve
notes by following links from related ideas — the way memory actually
works.
