## Linking notes creates a web of knowledge

Every permanent note should link to related notes using
[[double-bracket]] references. Links replace the need for
folders and hierarchies — instead of filing a note under
a category, you connect it to the ideas it relates to.

Links serve two purposes. First, they force you to think about
where a new idea fits in your existing knowledge — this act of
connecting is itself a form of understanding. Second, they make
the note denser with relevant terminology, which improves the
quality of its embedding vector.

The goal is a web, not a tree. A tree has one path to each node.
A web has many paths, mirroring how ideas actually connect.
