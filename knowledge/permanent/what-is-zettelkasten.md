## What is a Zettelkasten

A Zettelkasten is a personal knowledge management system invented by
German sociologist Niklas Luhmann. The name means "slip-box" in German —
a physical box filled with index cards, one idea per card.

Luhmann used his Zettelkasten to write over 70 books and 400 academic
papers across a 40-year career. He credited the system as his
intellectual partner, not just a filing cabinet.

The digital version replaces index cards with plain text files, one
file per atomic idea, linked together by references.
